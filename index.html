About

I am an applied economist and researcher working at the intersection of econometrics, political economy, and energy policy. I hold a BS in Applied Mathematics (summa cum laude, University Valedictorian) from the Ateneo de Manila University and am completing an MSc in Econometrics and Mathematical Economics at the London School of Economics.

Beginning in 2026, I will join the Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research (SIEPR) as a two-year pre-doctoral research fellow in energy economics. My work combines formal quantitative methods with questions of institutions, governance, and public policy. Alongside research, I have a long background in competitive debate and education outreach.

Research

The Families that Stay Together: A Network Analysis of Dynastic Power in Philippine Politics

Undergraduate thesis · 2025

A network-analytic study of political dynasties in the Philippines, measuring how kinship structures concentrate and reproduce political power over time. The paper applies tools from network science and quantitative political economy to a novel dataset of family and electoral linkages.

arXiv:2505.21280
