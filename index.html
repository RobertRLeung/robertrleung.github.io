About

I am a pre-doctoral research fellow at the Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research (SIEPR), where I study the economics of energy and digital markets. My research pairs formal quantitative methods with questions of market design, regulation, and public policy.

Before Stanford, I completed an MSc in Econometrics and Mathematical Economics at the London School of Economics and a BS in Applied Mathematics (summa cum laude, University Valedictorian) at the Ateneo de Manila University. Outside of research, I competed internationally in debate, winning the World Universities Debating Championship in 2023.

The Families that Stay Together: A Network Analysis of Dynastic Power in Philippine Politics

Undergraduate thesis · 2025

A network-analytic study of political dynasties in the Philippines, measuring how kinship structures concentrate and reproduce political power over time. The paper applies tools from network science and quantitative political economy to a novel dataset of family and electoral linkages.
